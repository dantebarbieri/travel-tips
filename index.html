Planning/Organization Apps

  • Automatically captures lodging/tickets from email.
  • Easy to access, share, etc.
  • You have to forward it your emails or let it watch everything that comes into your inbox.
  • Manually adding things that aren't detected via email can be painful.

I strongly suggest using apps like to organize lodging/tickets. Super helpful especially if you're going to be in a lot of Hostels or Airbnbs. Also great for getting to tickets and rail passes quickly.

Plan Enough to Always Have Something To Do

  • You're never at a loss for what to do.
  • Requires more planning.

If you don't plan enough, you'll end up wasting a lot of time trying to figure out what to do.

At the same time, minimize commitments. Try to not book things unless you're sure you want to do them.

I personally find that the best balance is struck when I plan some major things to do and also some things that would be fun in the nearby area (e.g. walks, parks, museums, etc.). Then, if I have extra time I can do some of the fun things but I never have any intention to do them so I don't feel to bad if I don't.

Know All Traveler’s Levels

  • You don't get tied down by the slowest traveler.
  • You don't get exhausted by the fastest traveler.
  • You may need to split up your group at times.

It is important when trip planning to make sure that everyone's expectations are aligned. My brother will be done after a single excursion per day and will also need rest days from time to time. My parents, conversely, will want to do multiple things per day and will not want to rest. Anjali and I are somewhere in the middle…

Get Travel Insurance

  • If something goes wrong you're covered.
  • Your regular insurance (medical, car, etc.) likely doesn't have international coverage.
  • Really not super expensive.
  • An extra expense.
  • You probably won't need it or use it.

We used to not get travel insurance for our trips because we figured that we didn't need it. Then, my father called his car insurance before we took a trip to Iceland because it would be the first trip outside of North America where we'd have a car. His insurance agent told him plainly that he would not be covered abroad, so he did some digging and got travel insurance.

You're covered for the following usually:

  • Accidental Death & Dismemberment
  • Emergency Accident and Sickness Medical Expense (+ Dental)
  • Trip Cancellation
  • Trip Interruption
  • Trip Delay
  • Repatriation of Remains
  • Non-Medical Emergency Transporation
  • Baggage/Personal Effects
  • Baggage Delay

All of the above cost Anjali & I a total of about $150 for our trip to Switzerland. It's a rather safe country and we're only gone for a little over a week, but the coverages are rather good in case something goes wrong and the expense is fairly minor. Plan Your Food

Especially if you have a picky eater in your party!

  • You get to enjoy the local cuisine from authentic eateries.
  • Can take a lot more planning and requires a certain skill to do well.

Anjali generally recommends going to local food blogs and reading reviews of their favorite restaurants with an emphasis on any dietary restrictions you may have. Because we're both vegetarian, this would be a really important step for our trips, except that I can find pizza anywhere.

My family never does this because none of us individually are foodies. My father and I have always had luck finding an Italian place, and food has never been a huge part of the trip for most of the family. My brother will eat anything with meat, and usually feels sad after not having steak for a long time. He often tries to get steak on trips without much success. Finally, my mom, who is the biggest foodie in the family will eat anything and is generally happy with wherever we go eat on trips.

If you're a foodie or food is a major part of the trip for you, then plan your food! Otherwise, you should only do this if you are really picky or have other dietary restrictions. Anjali uses local food blogs to plan trips. If you're looking to travel to Austin, TX, I can strongly recommend .

Luggage Management

  • Generally available.
  • Need to contact lodging ahead of time.

This is only necessary for trips where you're moving around a lot. If you're going to stay in one place for multiple nights, this is not a problem.

Double check that all of your lodgings will let you drop luggage there. Hotels are great at this, ’s vary by host (make sure to ask!), and I don't have as much experience with Hostels.
